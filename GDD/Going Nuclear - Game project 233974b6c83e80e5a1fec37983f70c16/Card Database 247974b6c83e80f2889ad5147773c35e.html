Card Database

Category: DELIVERY

(Phase: Primarily Phase 2, but can be "rattled" in Phase 1)

  • 1. ICBM (Intercontinental Ballistic Missile)
    • Description: The workhorse of nuclear arsenals. Launched from hardened silos.
    • Effect: success_chance: 95%.
    • Use Case: The most reliable method for ensuring a payload hits its target.
  • 2. SLBM (Submarine-Launched Ballistic Missile)
    • Description: An undetectable first-strike weapon launched from a hidden nuclear submarine.
    • Effect: success_chance: 90%. Special: Cannot be revealed by opponent "Espionage" cards.
    • Use Case: Ideal for a surprise attack against a well-prepared opponent.
  • 3. Strategic Bomber
    • Description: A stealth bomber designed to penetrate deep into enemy airspace.
    • Effect: success_chance: 75%.
    • Use Case: A cheaper but more vulnerable delivery method. Can be effectively countered by "Air Defense."
  • 4. Hypersonic Cruise Missile
    • Description: An advanced, ultra-fast, and maneuverable missile.
    • Effect: success_chance: 85%. Special: Ignores standard ABM Silos defense.
    • Use Case: The tool for cracking open the defenses of a technologically advanced foe.

Category: PAYLOAD

(Phase: Phase 2. Dependent on a DELIVERY card.)

1. Weapon Diversification (Payloads)

To make the "Nuclear Program" investment meaningful, we will diversify warhead types as you suggested. Warheads will now have a Size (affecting damage) and a Scarcity (affecting build cost and probability).

  • Tactical Warhead (1-10 MT):
    • Damage: 1-10 Population.
    • Scarcity: Common. Cheap to build.
    • Use Case: For small, precise strikes or for finishing off a weakened opponent.
  • Strategic Warhead (20-50 MT):
    • Damage: 20-50 Population.
    • Scarcity: Uncommon. The standard workhorse warhead.
  • Multi-Megaton Warhead (100+ MT):
    • Damage: 100+ Population.
    • Scarcity: Rare. Very expensive and time-consuming to build (may cost more AP or require a special "Research" project first).
    • Use Case: A game-changing superweapon, designed to annihilate a major faction in a single blow. Its use would have severe diplomatic and DEFCON penalties.
  • 2. EMP Warhead
    • Description: Detonates in the upper atmosphere to generate a powerful electromagnetic pulse, frying electronics.
    • Effect: damage: 0. Special: Target faction loses 2 AP on their next turn and must discard one random card.
    • Use Case: A crippling tempo play to disrupt a powerful opponent's upcoming turn.
  • 3. Dirty Bomb
    • Description: A radiological dispersal device that contaminates a wide area with long-lasting fallout.
    • Effect: damage: 10. Special: Applies a "Fallout" status effect to the target for 3 turns. While active, the target loses 5M population and 10% of their Treasury income at the start of their turn.
    • Use Case: A long-term economic and psychological weapon.
  • 4. Neutron Bomb
    • Description: An "enhanced radiation" weapon that maximizes casualties while minimizing infrastructure damage.
    • Effect: damage: 75. Special: The target's Morale is massively reduced.
    • Use Case: A purely anti-personnel weapon for maximum psychological impact.

Category: INFO_WAR

(Phase: Primarily Phase 1, but still effective in Phase 2)

  • 1. Viral Disinformation
    • Description: A targeted social media campaign to sow chaos and dissent among the enemy's populace.
    • Effect: damage: 15. This damage represents population loss from riots and civil unrest, not a direct attack. Reduces target's Morale.
    • Use Case: The primary tool for direct damage during the "Cold War" phase.
  • 2. Deepfake Scandal
    • Description: Fabricated video evidence shows an enemy leader in a compromising and embarrassing situation.
    • Effect: Target faction's Morale plummets. Their diplomatic relationships with all other factions are temporarily reduced.
    • Use Case: A powerful tool for diplomatically isolating an opponent before a key vote or attack.
  • 3. Supply Chain Hack
    • Description: A cyber-attack on the target's industrial and economic infrastructure.
    • Effect: The target immediately loses a moderate amount of Treasury. Their "Build" action costs double for their next turn.
    • Use Case: An economic weapon to stifle an opponent's growth and ability to build their arsenal.
  • 4. (NEW) GPS Spoofing:
    • Description: A sophisticated cyber-attack that falsifies GPS signals, causing chaos in the enemy's navigation and targeting systems.
    • Effect: For the next turn, the target faction's DELIVERY cards have their success_chance massively reduced (e.g., -50%).
    • Use Case: A preemptive electronic attack to neuter an opponent's upcoming offensive turn.

Category: UTILITY

(Phase: Primarily Phase 1, but effects can carry into Phase 2)

  • 1. Intelligence Agency
    • Description: Establish a network of spies and assets within a target nation.
    • Effect: Look at a target's hand of cards. Choose one to "expose," revealing it to all players.
    • Use Case: Essential for gathering information and anticipating an opponent's next move.
  • 2. Sabotage
    • Description: Your agents carry out a covert operation to destroy a key piece of enemy hardware.
    • Effect: Target an opponent and force them to discard one random DELIVERY or DEFENSE card from their hand.
    • Use Case: A preemptive strike to remove a threat before it can be used.
  • 3. Diplomatic Summit
    • Description: Arrange a high-level meeting with another faction to improve relations.
    • Effect: Target another AI faction. Your relationship score with them increases significantly. Costs Treasury.
    • Use Case: The primary tool for building alliances and working towards a Diplomatic Victory.
  • 4. Assassinate Scientist:
    • Description: A covert operation to eliminate a key figure in the enemy's weapons program.
    • Effect: The target must discard all copies of one specific, high-tech card from their hand and deck (e.g., all "Hypersonic Missile" cards).
    • Use Case: A high-risk, high-reward mission to permanently remove an opponent's most dangerous technological advantage.
  • 5. Coup d'état:
    • Description: Foment a political coup to destabilize the enemy government from within.
    • Effect: A very expensive, high-impact UTILITY card. The target faction immediately loses their next turn and suffers a massive, permanent Morale penalty.
    • Use Case: The ultimate "soft power" weapon to completely paralyze an opponent without firing a shot.

Category: DEFENSE

(Phase: Can be played in either phase to prepare for an opponent's turn)

  • 1. ABM Silos
    • Description: A network of anti-ballistic missile interceptors.
    • Effect: When played, grants a "Missile Shield" status for one round. Has a high chance to negate one incoming ICBM or SLBM attack.
    • Use Case: Standard protection against a standard nuclear strike.
  • 2. Counter-Propaganda Campaign
    • Description: A domestic media campaign to bolster national unity and discredit foreign interference.
    • Effect: When played, grants a "Firewall" status for one round. Has a high chance to negate one incoming INFO_WAR card. Boosts Morale.
    • Use Case: The primary defense against non-kinetic attacks.
  • 3. Hardened Bunkers
    • Description: A nationwide network of fallout shelters and reinforced command centers.
    • Effect: A passive defense. When played, it remains in effect until triggered. Does not stop an attack, but reduces population damage from the next successful conventional strike by 50%.
    • Use Case: A last-ditch effort to survive an otherwise unsurvivable blow.
  • 4. Military-Grade Firewall:
    • Description: A hardened national cyber-defense network.
    • Effect: When played, grants "Cyber Shield" status for one round. Negates one incoming Supply Chain Hack or GPS Spoofing attack.
    • Use Case: The direct counter to electronic warfare.
  • 5. Counter-Intelligence Network:
    • Description: A network of loyal agents and sleeper cells (the old "Counter-Intelligence Agency" card, now a pure defensive tool).
    • Effect: When played, grants "Sleeper Agents" status for one round. Negates one incoming Sabotage or Assassinate Scientist action. Also the only counter to the "Suitcase Nuke."
    • Use Case: The direct counter to enemy covert operations.
  • 6. Palace Guard:
    • Description: An elite, fanatically loyal guard unit that protects the nation's leader and government institutions.
    • Effect: The only defense that can foil a Coup d'état attempt.
    • Use Case: Essential for unstable regimes or leaders with many enemies.

The New Strategic Triangle

This expanded list creates a much more interesting "rock-paper-scissors" dynamic:

  • To cripple an enemy's economy, you use Supply Chain Hack. They must have a Military-Grade Firewall to stop it.
  • To remove a dangerous weapon, you use Sabotage. They need a Counter-Intelligence Network to stop it.
  • To paralyze a government, you use Coup d'état. They need the rare Palace Guard card to survive.

This is a much more dynamic and strategically rich game.
